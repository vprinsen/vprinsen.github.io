Mock duck ("duck") curry

Ingredients:

  • 1-2 cans veggie duck*
  • 400mL coconut milk
  • 3 kaffir lime leaves, torn
  • 2 tbsp red curry paste
  • 1 tbsp dark soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 1/2 tbsp maple syrup (original recipe calls for 15g palm sugar dissolved in 1 tbsp boiled water)
  • 1/4 fresh pineapple, chunked, or ~200mL canned pineapple chunks (drained of syrup)
  • 8 small cherry tomatoes
  • 12 lychees
  • Thai basil leaves
Serve with: rice

Preparation:

  1. Pan-fry the duck pieces of high heat until they are crisp and set aside.
  2. Pour the coconut milk, torn kaffir lime leaves, and red curry sauce into the pan and simmer 2 minutes, stirring constantly.
  3. Add the dark and light soy sauce and maple syrup. Simmer 2 more minutes.
  4. Add the pineapple and cherry tomatoes and simmer for 2 minutes.
  5. Add basil leaves, lychees, and duck strips. Turn off the heat and serve garnished with remaining lime leaves and basil. Serve with rice.


Notes

*Where to find veggie duck?
You can find canned "mock duck" in most Asian groceries: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mock_duck

In Montreal, you can also get an excellent veggie duck from Paradis Vegetarien
The package comes with 2 pieces, I use one for this recipe.

A non-exhaustive list of stores where you can find most of these ingredients:
  • Marché Kim Phat (Brossard and other locations)
  • Marché Kim Hour (5 minute walk from Metro Saint-Michel)
